Cost Tracker — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026

Cost Tracker ("the app") is a personal finance application that runs entirely on your own Android device. It has no server, no user accounts, and no operator-side database. This policy explains what data the app handles and how.

What data the app handles

  • Account and transaction data you enter manually, import from CSV files, or retrieve from your banks via Enable Banking.
  • Bank connection credentials — specifically the Enable Banking application key and OAuth session tokens needed to read your account data.
  • App settings such as your chosen home currency and app-lock preference.

Where your data is stored

All of the above is stored only on your device. Financial records live in a local database; credentials and tokens are held in encrypted storage backed by the Android Keystore. None of this data is transmitted to, or accessible by, the app's developer. There is no cloud sync and no backend.

Third-party access

  • Enable Banking — when you connect a bank, the app communicates with Enable Banking's API to authenticate and retrieve your account balances and transactions on your behalf. Their handling of that data is governed by their own privacy policy.
  • Frankfurter (exchange rates) — the app fetches public currency exchange rates. These requests contain no personal or financial information.

What the app does not do

  • No analytics, tracking, or advertising.
  • Your data is never sold, shared, or sent to the developer.
  • No third parties receive your data except as described above.

Your control

Because everything is stored locally, uninstalling the app removes all of its data from your device. You can revoke a bank connection at any time through your bank or through Enable Banking.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised through the app's public code repository.

Cost Tracker is a personal, non-commercial application.
